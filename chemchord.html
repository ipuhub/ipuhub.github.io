Problem Statement

Water is essential to life. It need not be spelt out exactly how important it is. Yet water pollution is one of the most serious ecological threats we face today. The wastewater consists of various organic and inorganic pollutants which when present above a certain limit, can cause biochemical and toxic effects to not only human beings but also to flora and fauna. Thus, treating this wastewater becomes a task of utmost importance.

The participants need to make small scale model of a wastewater treatment plant which could treat wastewater fast and effectively and is comparable to the primary treatment of wastewater in an orthodox treatment plant. The model which acts as the fastest filter first and produces clean water would be awarded the first prize. Additional advantage would be given to eco-friendly models.


Important Points

The model should fix into a box of size 100 cm * 100 cm * 100 cm.
Electronic items are allowed.
The abstract should contain the basic principle, design and advantages.
The poster should all the positives of the model describing, why your model has an upper edge over other models.
To participate in this event, one needs to register his team. The registering link would be uploaded on ChemChord’16 Facebook page.
TREASURE HUNT
CHEM - E - KHOJ
REGISTER

The event will be conducted in two rounds:

Round 1- Elimination

The first round will be carried out in the open area. The search parties will be searching the various clues in the complete university campus. Registered Teams will be given just one hour to find 6 clues. The top 2 teams with the maximum clues in that hour will be send to the next and final round. The registration link will be uploaded on the Facebook page of ChemChord’16.

Round 2- Finale

The final round is the cherry on the cake of this event. This round involves a murder mystery. The top two teams will be locked in separate rooms which would be identical to one another. Each room would contain marked coordinates of the body, murdered in mysterious circumstances. There would be crucial evidences left by the murderer in jiffy plus some mind boggling puzzles, riddles and questions which would help to find the right murderer. Each team would be given 1 hour to prove their mettle. The team completing the given task first, would be adjudged as the winner. So from the open to the closed, the teams will be tested to the ultimate. The clues will be from the sectors of Quantitative questions, Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics of 12th standard and observational skills.


Rules

Maximum members in a team can be 4.
Teams need to be from one department or college.
No on the spot registrations allowed.
Maximum 20 teams will be participating in the first round.
